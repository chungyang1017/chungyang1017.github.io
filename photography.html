No. 二 ・ Photography

Shashin 写真 寫真

Frontispiece ・ 扉頁

Photography has been a quiet companion to my academic life for nearly thirty years — a way of paying attention to what the world is doing.

Most of my work happens in the cities, mountains, and coastlines of Taiwan and Japan: the slow walking pace of a Taipei lane at dusk, the brittle hush of a Kyoto morning in late autumn, the light that hangs above the Tamsui river when the buildings on the far bank begin to pulse. I shoot mostly with Fujifilm digital, occasionally with a Leica M6 or M11 — and when a place feels too long to fit inside a single rectangle, I pick up the Xpan.

The work below is grouped into seven series — Street, Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter, Ocean, and Xpan — each shaped less by a deliberate project than by the rhythm of light and the seasons themselves. They form, together, a slow record of looking.
